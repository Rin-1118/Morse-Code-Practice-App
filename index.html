    Tutorial

    Turn uppercase letter shapes into dots and dashes

    Code-chart facts, observations from the reference video, and our learning mnemonics are identified separately.

    Reference Video

    How the reference video is used

    Directly observed in the video

    The description of the YouTube Short titled “【雑学】「モールス信号（アルファベット）」を解説【暗記】” lists the Morse code for letters A through Z. The visible video frame places one large uppercase letter in the center and shows its dots and dashes as white marks below it.

    Our instructional interpretation

    This tutorial adopts the video's side-by-side association of an uppercase letter and its code, then extends it with shape-based cues for recalling the order of dots and dashes. The individual mnemonic descriptions are our additions, not claims about the video.

    Letter To Rhythm

    From alphabet to keying rhythm

    A

    Selected

    A .-

    Confirmed from the video: A is dot-dash (.-). This matches the A-Z code list in the video description.

    Our learning cue: Begin A with a light dot, then use its supporting right leg to recall the longer dash.

    Facts

    Core rules

    Timing units

    ITU-R Recommendation M.1677-1 defines a dash as three dot units, a character space as three dot units, and a word space as seven dot units.

    Listening practice

    For practical recognition, learn each character as one sound pattern instead of counting separate dots and dashes.

    Role of these mnemonics

    Shape association is an entry point. The final goal is to recognize the complete sound pattern directly as a character.
